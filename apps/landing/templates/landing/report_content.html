Our Aptitude Assessment report contains the following:

  1. Innate characteristics
    Describes the person’s inborn character; primary and secondary, and the recommendation on how to communicate and handle the particular character.
    Understanding people’s character is very important in establishing good relationship and trust. Parents can save frustrations and be more accepting of their child, resulting in a stronger bond and healthier relationship.

  2. Natural Learning Capacity
    Total Finger Ridge Count (TFRC) indicates a person’s brain learning capacity. It represents the total number of ridges on our fingerprints. This indicates the inborn potential of a person to receive and process information at the same time. A TFRC of 70 billion will be satisfactory, yet the majority of people will have TFRC between 100 and 140 billion.

  3. Inborn learning method
    This indicates a child’s preferred way of learning: receiving, memorizing and storing information. It is crucial that parents are aware of their child’s learning style in order to provide proper guidance that is effective and efficient. A person that is aware of their best learning method is more confident and adapt easily to new environment.

  4. Congenital Intelligences
    Reveals what talent mother earth has given to every individual. Priority can then be made to sharpen one’s talent and excel in the field that they are born well at. Knowing our own talent boost our confidence and provide direction to shape our future. Avoid trial and error which can be costly financially and draining emotionally.

  5. Occupational suggestions
    Recommends which areas of occupation are suitable for an individual based on their inborn intelligences. One can choose which is the most appropriate according to one’s interest and passion.

  6. Bonus aptitude: The Four Main Quotients
    Unfolds the spread of one’s brain priority: Intelligence Quotient (IQ), Emotional Quotient (EQ), Adversity Quotient (AQ), and Creative Quotient (CQ).

  7. Appendix
    • The multiple intelligences: its characteristics and ways to strengthen
    • Major types of fingerprints and its characteristics
    • Basic understanding of the human brain
